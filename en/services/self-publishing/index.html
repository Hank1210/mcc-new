Book Publishing

Authors today have many different options for bringing their work to readers

Traditional Publisher

The publisher handles editing, cover design, marketing and distribution. Authors give up rights and receive only a share of revenues (royalties). Access is selective – publishers only publish a few manuscripts.

Literary Agents

Agents pitch manuscripts to publishers. They increase the chances of a publishing deal but take commissions and tie the author into longer processes.

Wholesale

Distribution via wholesalers who buy books in bulk and deliver to bookshops. Suited to higher print runs and titles with stable demand – wholesalers don't accept every title.

Distributors & Aggregators

Intermediaries between author and platforms. They often handle distribution to multiple shops simultaneously and offer services like ISBN assignment, billing and marketing support.

Amazon Seller Central

Books can be sold like any other product via the Amazon marketplace model. The author manages production, storage, shipping and marketing – for printed books only. The workload can be very high.

Amazon only
Amazon Advantage

Programme in which Amazon purchases and distributes printed books (consignment). The author supplies, Amazon handles distribution. Limited availability – suited to established providers or special editions.

Self-Publishing

There are many options for publishing a book

Our recommendation: Start simple and cost-effectively, then add further options.

Self-publishing means publishing your book independently of a traditional publisher – on your own terms, but with full control. With Kindle Direct Publishing (KDP) by Amazon, authors can publish both eBooks and print-on-demand titles worldwide – with no upfront costs. And there are further excellent options like BoD, Tolino Media etc. for the German market. Simply compare.

Self-publishing is the entrepreneurial alternative for authors who don't want to wait for the decision of traditional publishers, but want to actively place their work on the market. With the right strategy – from manuscript to marketing – self-publishing can become a real business.

Advantages
  • 100% creative freedom: cover, content, pricing
  • Direct access to millions of readers via the Amazon marketplace
  • Attractive royalty models (up to 70% on eBooks)
  • Fast market entry: publication often within days
Challenges
  • Full responsibility for editing, cover design and marketing
  • Building a readership without an established publisher network
  • Professional quality assurance is crucial for success
